The Story Behind Swapifhy

Founder Story

We bring back human connection, because social media and AI promised us connection but actually just made us more alone. We're more "connected" than ever and somehow more isolated, and I refuse to accept that as normal.

But I didn't build Swapifhy because it sounded like a cool startup idea. I built it because I've lived the problem. I once enrolled in a 50,000-rupee robotics course. At the end, I wasn't satisfied — instructors kept changing, I felt my investment was wasted. I started a Coursera course in May and still haven't completed more than 10% of it because it put me to sleep every time I put the lectures on.

But then I'd turn around and teach my friends design. Learn coding from classmates. Watch skills get exchanged everywhere around me... except on a platform actually built for it. I've watched talented people not learn things because they couldn't afford courses. And I've watched other talented people unable to monetize skills they already had. Both problems existed in the same space. I was done waiting for someone else to fix it. So I built it myself.

It's the world I wish I had when I started learning design, research, coding, everything. It's about making learning feel less lonely, bringing people together around actually helping each other grow.

This is me — Anwesha — building the world I needed. Whether it becomes the next big thing or not, I'm building it anyway.The waitlist is ready. The community is forming. The product works.The only thing missing is you.

Anwesha Ganji

Founder & CEO of Swapifhy
